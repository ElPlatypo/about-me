Building A Desktop Power Supply

I have always worked with electronic components, however i never owned a lab-grade power supply, with regulable voltage output and all the safety features. The other day i realized i had a spare ATX power supply that was previously on an old computer and i wondered if there was some way to use adapt it for my usecase.

After doing some research i found out that this idea had been already tested and validated by a few people online witch gave me the confidence to try it myself. Taking inspiration from their designs i created a design in blender that would fit my components, spaces and personal taste:

After the initial design was done i bought some fresh filament for my 3D printer and started making all the pieces that i would need, wich took about 3/4 days of printing, accounting for a few mistakes along the way

I then started looking online for all the small additional components that i would need, such as a buck boost converter, a usb hub, a few fuses and connectors and bought some to try out

Once this hell of a soldering job was done i validated that everything worked correctly and i was very pleased when everything did. I now have a pretty decent desktop psu, capable of outputting up to about 250W per lane (Buck boost, 12V, 5V, 3.3V).
